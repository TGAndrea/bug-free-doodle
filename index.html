Below follows a short introduction to the planets in our solar system.

Planets of our Solar system

Our solar system exists out of 8, formerly 9, planets. Up until 2006 Pluto was part of the main planets, but got voted off due to not meeting the third condition of what makes a planet a planet. Hover - or tab on mobile - over the "About planet" text to learn more about them!

About Mercury

Mercury is the first planet in our solar system. Meaning it also is the closest one to the sun! Like Earth it is a terrestial planet, yet is the smallest one in our solar system.

About Venus

Being the second planet in our solar system, Venus is about the same size as Earth, but due to being so close to the sun Venus is incredibly hot. Unlike Earth, Venus has a very weak magnetic field.

About Earth

Earth, our home planet, is the third planet in our solar system. It is located in what scientists call: the Goldilocks zone. This means Earth is located in the perfect spot for life to flourish; not too hot and not too cold.

About Mars

The fourth planet in our solar system is Mars. It is about the same size as Earth, but is a lot colder and hostile. Mars lacks Earth's gravitional field and is often subjected to enormous dust storms and temperatures reaching between -140 °C and 20 °C.

About Jupiter

The fifth and largest planet of our solar system is the gas giant Jupiter. This means Jupiter has no surface. Jupiter is well known for its great red spot which is a giant storm the size of the Earth.

About Saturn

Saturn, the sixth planet, is known for its magnificent rings. Like Jupiter, Saturn is also a gas giant.

About Uranus

The seventh planet, Uranus, is often the butt of many jokes. The axis of Uranus is tilted in a 98° angle, also known as obliquity. It is speculated this is due to the enormous gravitational pull Uranus is under from its larger neighbours.

About Neptune

The eight, and technically final planet of our solar system, is Neptune. Well known for its gorgues blue colour, Neptune is very similar to Uranus. Its blue colour is due to the high concentration of methane in its atmoshpere.

About Pluto

As explained above, the ninth planet Pluto no longer meets the condition to be called a planet. But it will always remain one in our hearts!
